In this section we extend the weighting scheme to estimating the marginal distribution of the outcome.

The following decomposition is flexible in $\Phi.$
We get different causal estimands $\E[\Phi(Y(1))]$,
for example, 
the population average of $Y(1)$
for 
$
  \Phi(Y)=Y
$, that is,
$
\E[Y(1)]
$.
Likewise we get the distribution function of $Y(1)$ at $t$
for 
$
  \Phi(Y)=
  \mathbf{1}_{(-\infty, t]}
  (Y)
$,
that is,
$
\P[
Y(1)\le t
]
$.
\begin{gather}
 \sum_{i=1}^{n}  
 w_i
 T_i
 \Phi(Y_i)
  - \E [ \Phi(Y(1)) ]
  =
  \frac{1}{n}
  \sum_{ i = 1 }^{n} S_i
    + R_0
    + R_1
    + R_2
    ,
\end{gather}
where
\begin{align*}
  S_i 
  &:= 
  \frac{T_i}{\pi_i}
 \left( 
   \Phi(Y_i) - \E [ \Phi(Y_i(1)) | X_i ]
 \right)
 +
 \left( 
   \E [ \Phi(Y_i(1)) | X_i ] - \E [ \Phi(Y(1))]
 \right)
 \quad
 \text{for}\ 
 i \in \left\{ 1, \ldots, n, \right\}
 ,
 \\
  R_0
  &:=
  \sum_{ i = 1 }^{n}
  T_i
    \left(  
      w_i - \frac{1}{n \pi_i}
    \right)
 \left( 
   \Phi(Y_i) - \E [ \Phi(Y_i(1)) | X_i ]
 \right)
 ,\\
  R_1
  &:=
  \sum_{ i = 1 }^{n}
    \left(  
      T_i

 ,
 \\
  R_2
  &:=
  \sum_{ i = 1 }^{n}
    \left(  
      T_i
      w_i - \frac{1}{n}
    \right)
 B(X_i)^\top \lambda
 \quad 
 \text{for}

 \lambda \in \R^K
 .
\end{align*}

We can even view
$
\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}
\sum_{i=1}^{n}S_i 
$
as an empirical process 
$
\mathbb{G}_n f
$
indexed over 

\begin{gather}
  f_\Phi(T,X,Y)
  =
  \frac{T}{\pi(X)}
 \left( 
   \Phi(Y) - \E [ \Phi(Y) | X ]
 \right)
 +
   \E [ \Phi(Y) | X ] 
   .
\end{gather}
If $\mathcal{F}=\left\{ f_\Phi \colon \Phi \in \ \text{some set}\right\}$
is $\P$-Donsker, the empirical process converges to a tight gaussian process.
Then the functional delta Method is applicable.
